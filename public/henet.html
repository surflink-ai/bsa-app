How Waves Are "Measured" Today

✗

Eyeball method: Surfers, photographers, and commentators guess. Californians exaggerate, Hawaiians downplay. Nobody agrees.

✗

Body-part scaling: Steudtner's 86ft world record used his lower leg as reference from a photo. Took scientists 18 months.

✗

Expert panels: WSL Big Wave Awards use committees who review footage. Subjective. Slow. Controversial.

✗

Offshore buoys: NOAA/CDIP buoys are 10-50+ miles offshore. They measure deep-water swell, not breaking wave face height.

Why This Matters Now

●

Olympic legitimacy: Surfing in Tokyo 2020, Paris 2024, LA 2028. Olympic sports need objective measurement. Gymnastics has CoP. Surfing has... feelings?

●

Record claims stacking up: Every big winter produces multiple "biggest wave ever" claims. No way to verify quickly. Creates controversy, not credibility.

●

Broadcast revolution: Surfing broadcasts need real-time data overlays like every other sport. Football has speed guns. Tennis has Hawk-Eye. Surfing has nothing.

●

Surfline's blind spot: 3.5M users trust forecast models, not ground truth. Their "wave height" is a prediction, not a measurement.
